Rules

General Rules

  1. All participants of the competition must be individually registered with an account on the competition server to participate.
  2. You may participate as an individual or as part of a team. Team size is limited to 4 members.
  3. Participants must register using their school email.

Participation Guidelines

  1. No illegal or unethical activity will be tolerated. This includes attacks on other teams, non-CTF related hacking, or use of unauthorized tools.
  2. Do not attempt to exploit any vulnerabilities outside of the CTF challenge environment.
  3. Collaborating with other teams is allowed but must be within the spirit of the event.

Technical Rules

  1. You are responsible for your own tools and environment. Only publicly available tools are allowed.
  2. Cheating (such as exploiting vulnerabilities in the CTF infrastructure) will result in penalization or disqualification.
  3. In case of any issues or doubts regarding the rules, contact the event administrators via Discord.

Prizes and Scoring

  1. Points are awarded based on the difficulty of challenges. The leaderboard will be updated in real-time.
  2. Prizes can only made available to active students of Canadian universities.
  3. The final winners will be determined at the end of the competition, and prizes will be awarded based on the final leaderboard.
  4. The decision of the event organizers regarding scores and challenges is final.
